End of Guide Path

I sincerely hope that you've enjoyed this introduction to Taiwan's history. I decided to only cover Taiwan's development up to Tsai Ing-wen's election, as going beyond that would be to fully enter the realm of current politics, a subject even more divisive than history.

I would greatly appreciate any feedback, opinion or initial reaction you would be willing to share. It helps me improve the site further.

If this experience has piqued your curiosity and made you interested in learning more about the unique history and politics of Taiwan than this website can offer, here are some works I personally recommend:

  • Taiwan: A New History by Murray A. Rubinstein et al is the most popular and extensive piece of literature available about the history of the island, following its journey all the way from its ancient aboriginal tribes, to the presidency of Chen Shui-bian.
  • Forbidden Nation by Jonathan Manthorpe is a more recent, more concise and digestable book about Taiwan's history. It still covers the most important landmarks in Taiwan's history, alongside several interesting details Manthorpe et al did not.
  • From Opposition to Power by Shelley Rigger, written in 2001 in the wake of Chen Shui-bian's election, gives a detailed account of the factionalism, intricacies and inner workings of the Democratic Progressive Party.
