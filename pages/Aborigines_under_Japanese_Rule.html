Aborigines under Japanese rule

As part of the Japanese administration's pursuit of authoritarian rule, they initiated a long and violent campaign to restrict the Aboriginal peoples' liberties. Keeping them under control was especially important to the Japanese, as the valuable camphor trees used in carpentry and medicine could only be found in the mountainous regions inhabited by Austronesians. Their efforts included establishing "Aborigines only" zones and subjugation via the deployment of military forces, which at one point even included an aerial attack and naval bombardments.

The Austronesians retaliated as well, killing 134 Japanese in one case in 1930.
